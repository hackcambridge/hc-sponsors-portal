Competitions and Events

Give hackers a challenge.

We are more than happy to offer advice and guidance as to what sort of events and competitions tend to go down well.

Themed Prize

Set whatever challenge you want to.

A themed prize can be for whatever challenge you want. This can include not just software engineering but also data analysis challenges, or design problems, for example. If you plan to run a workshop, it may be a good idea to tie it in with the competition — for example, if you were to run a competition centred around blockchain, giving a workshop on blockchain technology is likely to be well-received.

Register a Side Event

Give hackers a fun break from their hack.

A side-event can be anything you want! Examples include a code-in-the-dark competition; hosting a dinner for hackers before the night before the hackathon; or even just cup-stacking. We are more than happy to discuss any ideas you may have and will be as accommodating as we can be with them.

Run an API Prize

Encourage hackers to build on your product.

An API Prize is a sponsor-run competition based around one of your products. To clarify, whilst the API Prize was originally intended as a hack around a company's API, we have extended it to all software and hardware products produced by the sponsor.

Hackers always love to try out new technologies, and providing an API prize can encourage them to try yours! Past sponsors have seen continued adoption of their service after the event. The competition should highlight the most exciting aspects of your product whilst being open-ended enough to fit into a variety of projects.
